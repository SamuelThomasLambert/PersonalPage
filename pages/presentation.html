Randomization

Randomization can bring an element of surprise and variety to conversation classes. The unpredictable nature means students will need to adapt quickly to unpredictable situations. Randomization in classes was traditionally achieved through the use of dice, cards or paper materials. Technology simplifies the use of randomization in conversation classes but also greatly expands on what can be achieved with it. I will introduce 5 different activities that I created that use randomization, and discuss the advantage technology has over traditional methods of randomization.

    Traditional means of Randomization:

    • Dice

    Dice Activity: Modals of probability

    Instructions

    • Fill in columns with
      1. Names or personal pronouns
      2. Modals of probability
      3. a prediction
    • Roll the dice three times
    • For each through choose the result from the table and tell your partner
    • Write your partners results

    Cards

    Cards

    • You can make instructions based on:
      1. Numbers 1/13
      2. Suit 1/4
      3. Number and suit 1/52

    Paper materials

    Paper materials

    • T-shirts matching
      1. Make a stack of T-shirts face down (column 1)
      2. Scatter the remaining on the table
      3. Students take a T-shirt from the deck and begin to describe it in detail
      4. Other students compete to find the matching T-shirt
